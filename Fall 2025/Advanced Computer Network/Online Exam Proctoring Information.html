Exams in this course may be proctored electronically using “Respondus Lockdown Browser.” To protect against academic integrity violations, the softwre monitors, and in some cases records or analyzes, activity on your computer screen, your internet activity, and webcam/microphone. Monitoring is only active during an assessment.

If you are not comfortable with the use of Lockdown Browser in your home (or other private spaces), or installing Lockdown Browseron your personal computer, you should plan to complete software-proctored assignments and tests on a University-owned computer in a University computer lab. Optional pen-and-paper assessments may be available to students who opt-out of software proctoring. It is the student’s responsibility to notify me in writing at least twenty-four hours before a scheduled event if they wish to opt-out. Students who do not opt-out in a timely manner may choose to complete the software-proctored assessment, or accept an incomplete/zero for the assessment.

To complete a software-proctored assignment or exam, you must have a reasonably modern computer with a functioning webcam, microphone, and internet access. If you do not have suitable technology, you should arrange to complete the assessment in a University computer lab. 

Consult University Policy No. 2360 for complete details about the University’s use of software proctoring.
